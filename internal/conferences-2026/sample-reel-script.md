# Sample Reel Script + Production Notes

180-second cap. Phone-shot OK. Unlisted YouTube upload. Link in CFP submission.

---

## Why a sample reel matters

MicroConf and AfroTech both prioritize speakers who have proof of presence. The reel is not asking you to be polished. It is asking the program committee to see how you handle a camera, how you frame an idea, and whether you can hold attention for 60 seconds without slides.

AI Tinkerers does not technically require one, but it raises the demo's odds.

Same reel works for all 3.

---

## The 180-second structure

| Beat | Time | What |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | 0:00 to 0:05 | Direct-to-camera intro |
| Demo | 0:05 to 1:05 | Screen-record of one real agentsHQ pipeline firing |
| Why it matters | 1:05 to 1:35 | Direct-to-camera, why the demo is the point |
| Call to action | 1:35 to 2:00 | Direct-to-camera, single ask |
| Outro card | 2:00 to 2:10 | Static name + URL slate (optional, no audio) |

---

## Script

### Beat 1 (0:00 to 0:05) - Hook

Direct to camera, mid-shot, soft window light.

> "I am Boubacar. I build AI workflow stacks for solo consultants and small business operators. Let me show you one."

### Beat 2 (0:05 to 1:05) - Demo (screen-record, voiceover)

Pick ONE pipeline. The strongest demo candidates from the existing stack:

**Option A: `sequence_engine` cold-outreach loop** (recommended for MicroConf)
- Show the Notion content board with a queued prospect
- Show the Telegram approval message firing
- Show the Gmail "sent" confirmation
- Voiceover: "This is a single async loop. The agent drafts the email, pings me on Telegram, and waits. I approve or reject from my phone in five seconds. It sends. It logs the reply when one comes in. I never opened my laptop for this one."

**Option B: `studio_publisher` faceless-channel post fire** (recommended for AI Tinkerers)
- Show the Studio dashboard with a queued post
- Show the Blotato API call going out
- Show the live post on the brand channel
- Voiceover: "This is the publisher for one of three faceless brand channels I run. The whole flow from idea to live post is 12 minutes of agent time and 30 seconds of my time to approve. The same orchestrator runs three brands in parallel."

**Option C: `republish_queue` cross-platform fan-out** (recommended for AfroTech)
- Show one piece of content being routed to LinkedIn + X + YouTube Shorts
- Voiceover: "One source post. Three platforms. The agent picks the right format, the right hook, the right hashtags per channel. I review the proposals on Telegram. The system handles the rest."

### Beat 3 (1:05 to 1:35) - Why it matters

Direct to camera again.

> "The point is not the pipeline. The point is the pattern. One person can run what used to take a four-person team. The system is open. I document everything I build. If you are a solo operator and you have hit the wall where your time is the bottleneck, the stack underneath this is reproducible. That is the talk I want to give."

### Beat 4 (1:35 to 2:00) - Call to action

Direct to camera.

> "If you run a conference, a meetup, or a community where this would land, let me know. I bring real systems, real numbers, and the failure modes too. You can find me at catalystworks.consulting or on LinkedIn."

### Beat 5 (2:00 to 2:10) - Outro slate (optional)

Static frame, no audio.

```
Boubacar Barry
Catalyst Works
catalystworks.consulting
boubacar@catalystworks.consulting
```

## Production notes

**Camera:**

- Phone (iPhone 13+ or Pixel 7+) on tripod at eye level
- 4K 30fps if device supports, otherwise 1080p 30fps
- Lens: standard, NOT wide-angle (avoid the GoPro face)

**Audio:**

- Lavalier mic OR phone in landscape mode 18 inches from face
- AirPods Pro recording works if no lav is available
- Test audio FIRST. Record a 30-second sample, listen on headphones, fix any room echo before the real take

**Light:**

- North-facing window, mid-morning
- White wall or seamless backdrop behind subject
- NO overhead office light. It casts panda-shadow under eyes.

**Screen recording:**

- macOS: QuickTime screen recording, 1080p minimum
- Windows: OBS Studio
- Mouse cursor visible, highlight clicks if your OS supports it

**Edit:**

- DaVinci Resolve (free) or Descript (paid, faster for voiceover)
- Cut the umms and the half-sentences
- Hard cut between beats, no fancy transitions
- Captions burned in. 50% of viewers watch muted.

**Hosting:**

- YouTube unlisted (not private). Conferences need to view without an invitation.
- Add chapter markers at each beat
- Include the script in the description for accessibility

## Time budget

| Step | Time |
|---|---|
| Setup (camera, light, backdrop) | 30 min |
| Record direct-to-camera beats | 30 min (3 takes per beat) |
| Screen-record demo | 30 min (1 dry run + 2 real takes) |
| Edit | 60 min |
| Caption + upload | 30 min |
| **Total** | **3 hours** |

If 3 hours is too much, drop Beat 5 and shoot Beats 1 + 3 + 4 in a single take. That cuts editing in half.

---

## Anti-patterns (do NOT do these)

- Talking head against a busy bookshelf (visual noise)
- Dim "moody" lighting (reads as low-effort, not cinematic)
- Stock music underneath the voice (reads as low-effort marketing)
- Title cards with animated text wipes (reads as overproduced)
- Mentioning "10X" or "transform" or "revolutionize"
- Reading from a script visibly (eyes flicking off-camera)
- Asking the viewer to "smash that like button"
